# Breakfast and Cardiovascular Health Annotated Bibliography

This repository hosts a curated, version-controlled annotated bibliography focusing on the relationship between breakfast consumption habits and cardiovascular health markers.

## Scope

The bibliography includes research papers that investigate the impact of breakfast (or skipping breakfast) on various cardiovascular health outcomes in human subjects.

## Inclusion Criteria

- Papers must be published in English.
- Studies must involve human subjects (any age group).
- Research must examine a direct link between breakfast consumption (or skipping) and cardiovascular health markers, such as:
  - Blood pressure (systolic/diastolic)
  - Lipid profiles (total cholesterol, LDL, HDL, triglycerides)
  - Cardiovascular disease risk (incidence, mortality)
  - Inflammatory markers (CRP, IL-6, etc.)
  - Metabolic syndrome components
  - Endothelial function

- Both observational (cohort, cross-sectional) and interventional (randomized controlled trials) studies are eligible.
- Publication date: No restriction, but emphasis on recent (past 10 years) research.

## Exclusion Criteria

- Animal studies.
- Studies focusing solely on breakfast composition without cardiovascular health outcomes.
- Non-English publications.
- Reviews, meta-analyses, or editorials (unless they include original data).

## How to Contribute

1. Open an issue with the paper details.
2. Create a branch and add annotation to `annotated_bibliography.md`.
3. Submit a pull request for review.
